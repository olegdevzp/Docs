# Jimi Hendrix Style Learning Roadmap (Intermediate Level)

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### Technical Prerequisites
- Master the minor pentatonic scale in all positions
- Comfortable with basic barre chords (E and A shapes)
- Develop strong rhythm hand independence

### Key Focus
- **Thumb-over technique**: Practice fretting bass notes with your thumb
- **Chord embellishments**: Add hammer-ons and pull-offs to basic chords (E, A, D shapes)
- **Rhythm fundamentals**: Study "Hey Joe" and "The Wind Cries Mary"

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Problem: Thumb cramping with thumb-over technique**
- Solution: Reduce tension, practice in short bursts (5 min), gradually build strength

**Problem: Embellishments sound muddy**
- Solution: Slow down, ensure each note rings clearly before adding the next

**Problem: Can't keep time with backing tracks**
- Solution: Practice with metronome first, start slower than target tempo

**Problem: Fingers can't reach for chord embellishments**
- Solution: Stretch exercises before practice, work on finger independence drills

**Problem: Transitions between pentatonic positions feel awkward**
- Solution: Practice just the transition points, use visual markers (dots on fretboard)

## Phase 2: The Hendrix Sound (Weeks 5-8)

### Gear & Tone
- Understand Fuzz Face and Uni-Vibe effects
- Practice with overdrive/distortion
- Experiment with wah-wah pedal technique

### Technique Development
- **Double stops**: Bend two strings simultaneously
- **Vibrato mastery**: Wide, expressive vibrato on bends
- **String raking**: Muting and raking across strings
- **Songs to learn**: "Purple Haze" (rhythm parts), "Foxey Lady"

## Phase 3: Lead Playing (Weeks 9-16)

### Core Techniques
- **Hendrix bends**: Whole-step and 1.5-step bends with vibrato
- **Triad arpeggios**: Major and minor triads up the neck
- **Octave playing**: Double-stop octaves with palm muting
- **Sliding**: Smooth transitions between positions

### Study These Solos
- "Red House" (blues fundamentals)
- "Little Wing" (melodic phrasing)
- "All Along the Watchtower" (intensity and dynamics)

## Phase 4: Advanced Hendrix (Weeks 17-24)

### Complex Techniques
- **Chord melody**: Playing melody and harmony simultaneously
- **Backward guitar effects**: Understanding studio techniques
- **Feedback control**: Controlled feedback for sustained notes
- **Harmonic integration**: Natural and artificial harmonics

### Master These Songs
- "Bold as Love" (fluid legato playing)
- "Machine Gun" (wah technique and sustain)
- "Voodoo Child (Slight Return)" (complete package)

## Phase 5: Style Integration (Weeks 25-36)

### Musical Concepts
- **Blues vocabulary**: Mix Chicago blues with psychedelic elements
- **R&B rhythm guitar**: Curtis Mayfield-influenced chord work
- **Improvisation**: Apply Hendrix phrasing to your own solos
- **Stage presence**: Even the physical approach matters!

### Creative Application
- Jam over blues backing tracks (slow blues in E, A)
- Write original riffs inspired by Hendrix
- Learn complete performances: "Live at Monterey," "Band of Gypsys"

## Daily Practice Routine (60-90 minutes)

1. **Warmup (10 min)**: Pentatonic scales, finger exercises
2. **Technique focus (20 min)**: Work on current phase technique
3. **Song learning (20 min)**: Learn one section of a Hendrix song
4. **Improvisation (15 min)**: Apply techniques over backing tracks
5. **Ear training (10 min)**: Transcribe Hendrix licks by ear
6. **Creative play (15 min)**: Free jamming with new concepts

## Key Mindset Tips

- Hendrix was self-taught and experimental - don't be afraid to fail
- Focus on **feel and expression** over technical perfection
- Listen to his influences: B.B. King, Curtis Mayfield, Bob Dylan
- Record yourself regularly to track progress
- Play with others when possible
